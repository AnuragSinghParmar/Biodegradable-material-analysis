BIODEGRADABLE MATERIAL ANALYSIS



INTRODUCTION

Biodegradable materials are substances that can be broken down by natural processes into

simpler compounds, like water, carbon dioxide, and biomass, without causing harm to th

environment. They play a crucial role in addressing environmental concerns related to plastic

waste and pollution. Analyzing biodegradable materials involves various aspects, including their

composition, degradation behavior, and environmental impact.

THE SCIENCE OF BIODEGRADABLE MATERIAL

Biodegradable materials are substances or products that can be broken down naturally by

microorganisms (bacteria, fungi, etc.) or other living organisms into simpler, environmentally

benign substances, such as water, carbon dioxide, and biomass. These materials are designed

to decompose in a way that does not harm the environment and can reduce the burden of

waste in landfills and ecosystems. Biodegradable materials can be derived from both natural

and synthetic sources

FOOD WASTE

Organic materials, such as food scraps, yard waste, and natural fibers, are naturally biodegradable and can be composted to create nutrient-rich soil.

BIODEGRADABLE PACAKAGING

Packaging materials such as biodegradable bags, containers, and packaging films are often used to reduce plastic waste. They can be made from various biodegradable plastics or natural materials like paper or cardboard.
